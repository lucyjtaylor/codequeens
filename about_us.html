Who Are We?

The idea for Support Sisters began out of a conversation between 3 young women about how prevalent the issue of sexual harrassment and assault is in the UK, particularly the city of London. So many of us fear for their bodies and safety whilst navigating this beautiful city, but are at times ignored or met with disbelief. This can render many of us unable or unwilling to share. We decided to create a platform for women to share their stories, in an effort to uplift and acknowledge each other's shared experiences.

Knowledge Is Power

Our vision here at Support Sisters isn't just to provide a sounding board for our experiences. We also want to inform, educate and facilitate discussion. In order to understand our rights, it's so important to understand the facts. That is why we're also committed to bringing you clear, balanced information regarding consent, sexual ethics, assault,politics and the law. On the website you will find this in the form of articles, videos and blog posts.

Reporting the Crimes

The sad reality remains that, although studies show that 1 in 5 women aged 16-59 has experienced some form of sexual assault since the age of 16 - single or ongoing - only around 15% of indivuduals who experience sexual violence ever choose to report it to the police. This is why Support Sisters places a huge emphasis on equipping and encouraging victims to report sexual harrassment and assault. We take away the mystique of the process and provide a step by step guide on how to do this.

December Feature: #ThisIsNotConsent

This month's feature item is the protests that have arisen in Ireland and beyond after a defense lawyer, during the trial of a 17-year-old girl allegedly raped by a 27 year old, held up her lacy underwear in court to indicate her consent to the assault. The defendant was unanimously acquitted by a jury of 8 men and 4 women on 6 November.
